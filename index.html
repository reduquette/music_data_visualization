What years are the most songs in the Top 500 from?

The graph below shows how many of the Top 500 songs were made in different year intervals. Hover over a bar to see a list of the songs made during that time period.

The color scale below reflects the amount of songs. The more songs in a certain time period, the darker the blue becomes.

No matter what size time periods you look at, the 60s and early 70s hold the highest counts of the Top 500 songs. Songs from the 90s and 2000s combined barely make up 10% of the Top 500 songs.

How have songs changed in acousticness, danceability, and energy since 1948?

The blue line represents the acousticness of songs. As you can see, the amount of acousticness in songs decreases to almost zero.

The yellow line represents the energy of songs. Perceptual features include dynamic range, perceived loudness, timbre, onset rate, and general entropy.

The pink line represents the danceability of songs which is how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity.
