Through the following visualizations we will investigate the truth to the claim that "Modern music sucks".

What years are the most songs in the Top 500 from?

The graph above shows how many of the Top 500 songs were made in different year intervals. By changing the interval, you can see how many songs were created every year, 2 years, 5 years, etc. Once you hover over a bar, you are shown a list of the songs made during that time period.

The color scale above reflects the amount of songs. The more songs in a certain time period, the darker the blue becomes.

No matter what size time periods you look at, the 60s and early 70s hold the highest counts of the Top 500 songs. Songs from the 90s and 2000s combined barely makeup 10% of the Top 500 songs.

How have songs changed in acousticness, danceability, and energy since 1948?

❮ ❯
1 / 5

The blue line represents the acousticness of songs throughout the decades. The confidence measure from 0 to 1 of whether the track is acoustic. 1 represents high confidence the track is acoustic. The amount of acousticness in songs had a low dip in 1960 and then went to its max in 1970 before dropping to almost zero.

The yellow line represents the energy of songs throughout the decades. Energy is measure from 0 to 1 and represents a perceptual measure of intensity and activity. Typically, energetic tracks feel fast, loud, and noisy. For example, death metal has high energy, while a Bach prelude scores low on the scale. Perceptual features contributing to this attribute include dynamic range, perceived loudness, timbre, onset rate, and general entropy. There was a major dip close to zero in 1970 and from there steadily increases with only a small dip in 1990.

The magenta line represents the danceability of songs throughout the decades. Danceability describes how suitable a track is for dancing based on a combination of musical elements including tempo, rhythm stability, beat strength, and overall regularity. A value of 0 Is the least danceable and 1 is the most danceable. The danceability has remained fairly steady with only a dips in 1970 and a peak in 1990.
